Dr. Summer McLaughlin

Postdoctoral Fellow • Astrophysicist • Active Galactic Nuclei

About Me

I am a postdoctoral fellow in astrophysics specialising in active galactic nuclei (AGN), black hole accretion, and multiwavelength variability. I did my master's degree at the University of Bath where I worked with Dr. Carolin Villforth, and my PhD at the University of Sheffield in the UK working with Dr. James Mullaney.

Research

My research explores AGN accretion physics, variability timescales, and feedback mechanisms. I use a combination of observational data (X-ray, optical, IR), spectral analysis, and theoretical modeling. My broader interests include AGN unification, SMBH demographics, and the role of AGN in galaxy evolution.
