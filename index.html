About Me

I am a British postdoctoral fellow in astrophysics specialising in active galactic nuclei (AGN), black hole accretion, and multiwavelength variability. I did my master's degree at the University of Bath where I worked with Dr. Carolin Villforth, and my PhD at the University of Sheffield working with Dr. James Mullaney.

Research

My research focuses on the characterisation and modelling of variability in AGN, and how astroinformatics and advanced statistical techniques such as machine learning can revolutionise time-domain astronomy. More specifically, I am interested in the systematic detection and classification of extreme or rare forms of AGN variability (AGN flares), and using large time-domain surveys to uncover the physical processes driving these events.
